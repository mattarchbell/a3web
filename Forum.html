Frequently Asked Questions

The online graduate GIS Graduate Program at Fleming College is a full time course and the workload is quite intense. You need to dedicate a lot of time outside classes for assignments and studying to be able to make it through the course successfully.

As the GIS program is full time and the workload is demanding outside of classes, students find it hard to work a job alongside. Students who are not working, or are working very little hours find that they have more success at completing the work and time management.

The online GIS Graduate program has been successfully running online for many years so it is well formatted for delivering content through an online structure. Classes are delivered from the teachers themselves and are designed to be interactive. There is some group work involved throughout the course which is a great chance to meet new people, even though you are not there in person.

You will need some degree of software knowledge and understanding. You will be using a lot of new software related to GIS which you are taught, however basic computer knowledge is required. A lot of people who join this course from a variety of backgrounds, from Environmental to creative design, so you do not need a GIS background. There are of course some backgrounds that may tie in quite well with some of the contents of this course!

The applications specialist will dive deeper into data management. The cartographic specialist on the other hand, will focus more on the visuals.

You will be learning a whole host of skills that are commonly used in the GIS field. You will be covering areas suchs as Remote sensing, Problem Solving, Database Principles, Geovisualization, Spatial Analysis and web design! The website you are looking at right now was made using the skills learn in the first term of the program. How neat is that?
